The Good News & The Bad News

The Bad

Utility companies are monopolies that have complete control over pricing and service. When was the last time a monopoly was good for you and your family?
Utility prices continue to increase. Since 2000, the cost of electricity in America has increased by a whopping 64%.
As traditional sources of energy continue to get more expensive (including coal, gas, and oil), electricity rates will continue to rise.
We cannot continue to burn things to meet our energy needs. Past solutions are simply not sustainable for future generations.

The Good

Stop renting energy. Own it and build equity in your energy source instead.
Save money on your energy bill and build credits by contributing electricity to the grid.
Increase the value of your home by as much as $68,080.
Eliminate the equivalent of 16,980 gallons of gasoline consumed and contribute the benefits of 2,500 trees planted over the life of your system.
Reduce your carbon footprint by 2,500 pounds of CO2 emissions over the life of your system.
